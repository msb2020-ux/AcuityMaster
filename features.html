Units of Measure

AcuityMaster displays visual acuity in all popular formats. Switch between US, Metric, LogMAR, and Decimal instantly — no setup required between patients.

  • US — 20/200, 20/100 … 20/20, 20/15, 20/10
  • Metric — 6/60, 6/6
  • LogMAR — 1.0 through 0.0 (ETDRS standard)
  • Decimal — 0.1 through 1.0

Calibration

The calibration (setup) screen lets you configure AcuityMaster once for your exam lane. Set your preferred unit of measure, the distance between the display and the patient, and which fixation video clip to use for pediatric patients.

  • Unit of measure selection
  • Patient-to-screen distance (feet or meters)
  • Pediatric fixation video selection

Chart Types

In addition to the standard Snellen chart, AcuityMaster offers a full suite of specialist tests — every chart you need without switching applications.

  • Snellen — the standard acuity chart
  • ETDRS — Early Treatment Diabetic Retinopathy Study chart
  • Worth-Dot-Four — color-correct, calibrated for accurate results
  • Astigmatic Dial — Jackson cross-cylinder axis refinement
  • Fixation Disparity — binocular vision testing
  • Contrast Sensitivity — assess beyond Snellen acuity

Optotypes

In addition to standard Snellen letter optotypes, AcuityMaster provides alternative optotypes to assess visual acuity across a broad range of patients — including pre-literate children and non-English speakers.

  • Letters — standard Sloan/Snellen alphabet
  • Pictures — updated recognizable images, including a cell phone (we retired the rotary telephone!)
  • Tumbling E — for patients who cannot name letters
  • Landolt C — ISO standard broken ring
  • Numbers — Arabic numerals

Display Mode

Users can instantly change among several display modes without leaving the chart. Choose the presentation that best matches your clinical workflow and patient needs.

  • Single Letter — isolate one optotype, eliminate crowding effect
  • Single Line — display one full acuity line
  • Multiline — full chart view, traditional Snellen format

Pediatric Friendly

AcuityMaster is designed with pediatric patients in mind. Updated optotype pictures include images children actually recognize today — including a cell phone, because today's kids no longer recognize the rotary telephone.

A hands-free USB foot pedal lets you toggle the fixation video clip without leaving the patient — perfect for keeping a pediatric patient fixated at distance while you complete refraction.

  • Updated recognizable picture optotypes
  • Tumbling E and Landolt C for pre-literate patients
  • Hands-free video fixation via keyboard or USB foot pedal
  • Multiline pediatric chart view

Options

AcuityMaster offers a broad range of display and testing options that let you tailor each exam without switching applications or reconfiguring your setup.

  • Mirror mode — display behind patient via mirror, or standard (in front)
  • Toggle 20/XX label — show or hide the acuity notation on screen
  • Crowding bars — add surround optotypes to isolate crowding effect
  • Reduced contrast — multiple contrast levels for sensitivity testing
  • Red/Green overlay — for binocular balance and duochrome testing

Color Vision

AcuityMaster includes color vision screening as part of its comprehensive test suite. Color vision defects are common and often go undetected without dedicated testing — now integrated seamlessly into your digital chart workflow.

  • Color vision screening plates
  • Integrated with the standard exam workflow
  • No separate equipment or software required
  • Calibrated display for accurate color reproduction

Special Features

AcuityMaster includes practical features designed around real exam-lane workflows — especially useful for practices with dual-monitor setups or pediatric patients.

  • Undockable/Moveable Menu — for dual-monitor setups, undock the AcuityMaster control bar and drag it to the monitor closest to you. Full menu access without squinting at a display 15–20 feet away.
  • USB Foot Pedal — hands-free control of the pediatric fixation video. Keep your patient fixating at the end of the room while you complete refraction, without touching the keyboard.
  • Pinhole Acuity — simulate pinhole testing with an on-screen overlay.
  • Runs on existing hardware — compatible with Windows XP through Windows 11, including the computer already in your exam lane.
